    üìò PART VI ‚Äî OPERATIONAL PROJECT PROTOCOL

    The Operational Project Protocol is the tool that ensures every intervention executed under the Piregal‚ÄìHerculan alliance is a surgical operation: predictable, traceable, auditable, and legally shielded. In a sector where improvisation is the norm and errors are costly, this protocol sets a standard that admits no deviations. It describes what is correct, not "what is common."

    1. LOGISTICS, MOBILITY, AND STAFF MANAGEMENT

    Logistics is part of quality. A delay, poorly housed team, or missing tool backup can compromise an entire project. The alliance establishes a professionalized mobility model, worthy of an elite technical intervention team.

    • Team Mobility: Vehicles differentiated by type (technical vans for intervention and executive vehicles for meetings). Mandatory GPS tracking to optimize routes and coordination. International travel protocols for strategic projects (France, Italy, Morocco).
    • Accommodation and Well-being: Overnight standards defined by distance to the site and rest conditions to ensure performance during continuous application days and critical windows.
    • Insurance and Permits: Management of port passes, professional cards (DPA, ISPS), and restricted access. Verification of specific Liability Insurance for high-value assets (superyachts, hospitals).

    2. PREPARATION, SCENARIO, AND PROTECTION (THE SHIP'S SHIELD)

    In the naval sector, the environment is as critical as the product. Preparation is an act of audit, not a formality.

    • Entry Audit and ‚ÄúNotice of Readiness‚Äù: Area reception report signed with the shipyard. Confirmation of an area free of dust, moisture, and interference. Photographic documentation of prior damage to avoid false claims.
    • Luxury Perimeter Protection: Professional masking and watertight sealing of sensitive areas (wood, steel, upholstery, electronics). Installation of forced extraction in confined areas.
    • Surgical Substrate Preparation: Technical selection of abrasives (shot blasting, milling, or diamond) according to substrate. Final cleaning with industrial vacuuming and specific Herculan solvents.
    • Precision Environmental Control: Continuous monitoring with data loggers (wet bulb, dew point, surface temperature). Absolute prohibition of application if +3¬∞C above the dew point is not met.

    3. TOOLS, EQUIPMENT, AND REDUNDANCY

    On a superyacht, a broken mixer is a contractual disaster. We establish a model of critical redundancy.

    • ‚ÄúLevel 0‚Äù Tool Kit: Double-helix mixers, calibrated trowels, nylon spiked aerating rollers, and high-precision digital scales with mandatory calibration.
    • Critical Redundancy: Mandatory duplication of essential machinery (mixers, vacuums, extraction systems). Immediate replacement protocol in case of breakdown.
    • Maintenance: Immediate cleaning with authorized solvents. Dirty tools are grounds for immediate technical sanction.

    4. QUALITY MONITORING AND DIGITAL TRACEABILITY (ITP)

    Traceability is a contractual weapon to prove the alliance did the right thing.

    • Batch Inspection: Recording of batch numbers for each drum, linked exactly to the area of the ship where it was applied.
    • Layer and Timing Control: Marking re-coating times to guarantee chemical adhesion. Micron measurement in wet and dry states (ultrasonic on metal substrates).
    • Cloud Project Diary: Daily photographic report with shared access for Herculan and the final client. Documentary evidence for any dispute.

    5. STRATEGIC RELATIONSHIP WITH THE SHIPYARD

    Piregal acts as a technical authority, not as a subordinate trade.

    • Single Interlocution: The Piregal Site Manager is the sole contact for the shipyard Project Manager to eliminate conflicting orders.
    • ‚ÄúNotice of Delay‚Äù Protocol: Immediate issuance of an official document if another trade invades the area or if environmental conditions are unfit. Transfers responsibility for the delay to the shipyard.
    • Waste Management: Removal of packaging and chemical waste according to MARPOL regulations and local legislation.
